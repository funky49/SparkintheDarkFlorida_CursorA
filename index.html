Projects

Oobleck Dance Generator basslab.science

Idea: Make it easier to play with Oobleck (a non-Newtonian fluid) and bass

An interactive educational experience that bridges technology and physical science. Using a custom web application, basslab generates specific low-frequency sounds designed to interact visually and physically with non-Newtonian fluids (like oobleck). This provides a striking, hands-on demonstration of cymatics and sound wave energy.

About

Mission: Spark in the Dark Florida is dedicated to non-commercial research and the dissemination of scientific information for public education and general welfare. We look to engage others via "Do It Yourself" and hacker ideals with hands-on fun. We are going to give entropy a battle.

Hello from Steve Rush! I am the founder of Spark in the Dark Florida. My background is rooted in the nexus of technology, music, and science.

I started this organization because I believe scientific literacy is crucial for the general welfare of the public. By translating complex ideas into interactive, understandable experiences, we can spark curiosity and a lifelong love of learning.

I have presented for the National Fermi Accelerator Laborator and the National Academies of Science. I have also used hip-hop as a tool for science outreach. I have rapped for The Planetary Society, the National Science Foundation, the USA Science & Engineering Festival, and the St. Petersburg Science Festival.

Wired magazine named me a "Nerdcore Hip-Hop All Star". I have proudly performed at geek-friendly concerts such as Nerdapalooz and the Orlando Nerd Fest.

Vision: Our vision is to plot, plan, stratigize, organize, and mobilize pro-science ideas. Current vectors include:

  • Developing software
  • Creating exhbits for science museums and festivals
  • Organizing pop-up science experiments
  • Deploying vending machines with DIY science experiences
  • Research and experimentation
  • Presenting educational content
  • Teaming with other organizations
